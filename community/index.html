Our Mission

Te Whainga

Technology continues to influence the world of work.

This means immense opportunity for future careers and learning, but also an increased need to ensure our people are well-equipped to get involved.

We believe to effectively support growth opportunities in the tech sector, it needs a community-centric approach that bridges transitional gaps between individuals, education, industry and government, while upholding the hauora (wellbeing) of potential Contributors.

'Kupenga' is a pilot Te Ao Māori mentoring initiative we're developing to make it easier for our people to contribute to Open Source and position Aotearoa in the future of Cloud.

Our Values

​Whanonga pono

The New Zealand Government estimates that up to one in five New Zealanders experience some form of digital exclusion due to a lack of access, affordability, skills, trust, or the motivation to get online - Salmon, Kaleah. 2023

Through our initiatives, we aim to:

Empower underepresented groups:

  • We are passionate about increasing the representation of Māori and females in the tech space. By providing support, mentorship, and opportunities, we seek to bridge the diversity gap and empower underrepresented individuals to thrive in tech careers.

Foster Collaboration

  • We believe that collaboration is key to unlocking the full potential of technology. Our community serves as a hub for like-minded individuals to connect, share knowledge, and work together on cloud-native, open-source projects that push the boundaries of innovation.

Promote open source principles

  • Open source lies at the heart of our community. We advocate for the free exchange of ideas and knowledge, enabling continuous learning and collective progress in the tech industry.
